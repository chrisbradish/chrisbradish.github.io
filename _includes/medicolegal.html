About

I worked as a Consultant Orthopaedic Surgeon specialising in children’s orthopaedics from 1988 until 2014. I initially worked at the Children’s and Royal Orthopaedic Hospitals in Birmingham from 1988 to 2007 and then at Great Ormond Street Hospital, London, where I was the senior orthopaedic surgeon until my retirement from NHS practice at the end of 2014. Since then I have continued to undertake medicolegal work, exclusively relating to children’s orthopaedics and the adult sequelae of childhood conditions.

I have extensive experience of all aspects of children’s orthopaedics, with particular interests in congenital deformities, limb reconstruction, cerebral palsy and other neuromuscular conditions, hip and foot disorders. I am a Past President of the British Limb Reconstruction Society (BLRS) and was President of the British Society for children’s Orthopaedic Surgery (BSCOS) 2008 to 2010. I am a former council member of the British Orthopaedic Association (BOA). I remain an Honorary Consultant at Great Ormond Street.
